Ramen (ラーメン)

Ichiran Ramen

Dinning at Ichiran Ramen makes for a very private, but nonetheless enjoyable Ramen experience. This is arguably the best, authentic ramen experience one could wish for in Japan, and that is at Ichiran Ramen.

Sushi (寿司)

Sushiro (スシロー)

This is a quick remedy for any cravings for sushi! Sushiro is a sushi restaurant franchise that specializes in serving sushi on a conveyor belt. Not only is it fast, it is also one of the cheapest options for authentic, and freshly made sushi. The sushi only gets made once you make an order, and you can expect it to arrive on a plate riding the conveyor belt in a matter of minutes!


This is a rather popular destination for sushi runs even among Japanese people. While you can hardly classify the sushi here as top-tier, the taste and variety of sushi that Sushiro provides is definitely worth trying out!

Quick Bites (comfort food)

Saizeriya (サイゼリヤ)

Saizeriya, or Saize for short, is one of the most well known family restaurant chains in Japan. Saizeriya is the definition of at-home style comfort food in Japan. Due to its Italian origins, Saizeriya offers a variety of western hot foods such as spaghetti, pizza, in addition to many Japanese meals. Furthermore, they offer an unlimited drink bar to customers. For this reason, you can see Japanese students frequent Saize in groups to dine and do group study sessions at the same time!

Sweets

Marion Crepes (マリオンクレープ)

Marion Crepes is a pioneer in the Japanese crepe industry, and their crepe stands offer ice cream crepes that will most certainly satisfy your sweet tooth. They have shops all over the country, and it is definitely worth a try if you just happened to pass one by. You always have space for desserts.
