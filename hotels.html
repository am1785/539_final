Capsule Hotel (カプセルホテル)

Hotel Zen Tokyo (30 - $40 USD /night)

Capsule hotels are a great choice for an economic, and comfortable stay!


While traveling across multiple regions of Japan, capsule hotels could provide one with the much needed hospitality to just stay the night. Hotel Zen and many other hotels alike provide guests with lockers to store their personal items, bath amenities to allow guests to freshen up, as well as a single bed tightly enclosed by a room (capsule) for guests to sleep in.


Capsule hotels could be found everywhere in the cities, and travelers could fall back to it when the need for a temporary stay arises. For example, it might be a good idea to stay at a capsule hotel in between flights/transit in Japan.

Traditional Inn / Ryokan (旅館)

Various (40 - $150 USD /night)

Traditional Inns allow guests to experience the full Japanese lifestyle and hospitalities.


Staying in Ryokans had been the traditional way for visitors to find a place to sleep. The first of such inns was established in 705 A.D.


Ryokans often provide traditional Japanese cuisine to their patrons. In addition, guests are housed in rooms with tatami flooring, and are given traditional Japanese clothing (yukata) during their stay.


The costs for staying at Ryokans nowadays varies greatly due to its scarcity in metropolitan areas. You are more likely to find affordable ones in rural areas in Kyoto.

Modern Inn (Hotels)

Hotel Palace Japan (~$40 USD/night)

Hotel Palace Japan is a fantastic choice for an affordable and economic living experience while touring Tokyo.


Located within 5 minutes of walking distance from the Minowa subway station, Palace Japan allows visitors of Japan the opportunity to stay connected with the most well known parts of Japan's capital without having to break the bank.


With that said, the amenities are modern and generally fantastic! The rooms, while small, are cleaned daily and comes with a functional TV set. Shower and bathroom facilities are shared with the entire floor, but the fact that they provide coin laundry services on the first floor is definitely a pleasant surprise. On top of that, the staff is extremely friendly, and they speak a decent amount of English!

Luxury Hotel

Imperial Hotel Tokyo (~$230 USD/night)

High-end hotels like the Imperial Hotel of Tokyo are landmarks to the city.


While it may be very costly, staying at such hotels would allow one to fully explore the very best of Japan's hotel industry.
